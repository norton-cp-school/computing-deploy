Try It Out

On a white board or piece of paper, draw two grids (at least 3x3) and mark some cells alive.

Remember the rules of Life:

  1. If a cell is alive, it only survives when it has two or three live neighbours.
  2. If a cell is dead, it only comes alive when it has exactly three live neighbours.

Look at each cell in turn and work out whether it will be alive or dead in the next generation. Mark your second grid with the result.

If you get on OK, try drawing another generation into another grid.

By this time, you’ll be thinking that we should play this on the computer.
